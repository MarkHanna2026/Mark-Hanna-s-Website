UMass Boston Diversity

UMass Boston ranks as the third most diverse university in the United States. There are a lot of students from everywhere in the world. They all bring different cultures to UMass, which is amazing; people learn about each other and their cultures, religions, and interests. Imagine visiting all over the world while being in one place, this is what I perfect. It's truly an amazing experience. Hosting cultural or religious events to make students feel at home, especially international students who live far away and can't experience their holidays and miss their traditions back at home. UMass Boston doesn't fail when it comes to making you feel at home where you belong, and cares about every one of their students.

UMBBeInvolved

For example, spirituality has a catholic student association that is for the Catholic community on the campus and also has bible study, prayer, and more. For ethnic/cultural, you have the African Student Union, arab student association, and black student center. There is something for everyone, no matter where you're from or what your beliefs UMass has it to enhance the quality of the students and make them feel at home.

Disability/ Mental Health

If you have a disability, do not worry, UMass has your back with its Ross Center for Disability Services. The goal is to help students with disability have an equal education level and to help people have a smooth college experience like everyone else by offering resources, assistive technology training, academic accommodations, and information to raise awareness of disabilities among university community members. If you struggling with mental health, there is a counseling center that helps anyone regardless of their sexual orientation, background, or beliefs, who struggles and needs counseling, and everything is private; the records will not be public. All of these services are completely free, which shows how UMass cares about the well-being of its students, and if you ever are stressed, they are here for you. That's why UMass Boston is for everyone.

Academic Support

UMass Boston offers a lot of Academic options to help you succeed. If you struggling with transitioning from high school to undergraduate school or undergraduate to graduate, or looking for an internship. Do not worry, UMass has your back and will provide support throughout the Academic Support & Success program. They also have a tutoring program where you can pick any classes you are struggling with and receive one-on-one tutoring. There is also your advisor who can help you with general academic advice, guide you on where to go if you need any help or graduation planning, concerns about any course, your resume, transfer course credit, and much more. Anything you want in terms of academic support, UMass Boston has. It makes the college experience smooth and takes off stress; you always know someone is there to help you succeed.
