Colleges & Tracks

UMass Boston offers 4 tracks for the two colleges, which are college of management and college of science and mathematics. The outcome for the college of management: IT Professional, IT Manager, Business Systems, Analyst Consultant, Data Analyst, and CTO. The outcome for Bachelor of Science: IT Consultant, Business Intelligence Analyst, Project Manager, Cloud Solutions Architect, Systems Analyst, and Computer Forensics Investigator. The four tracks are system administrator, information architecture, business intelligence, and computer forensics. UMass Boston prepares you to become the best in the field of IT. Also, the hands-on projects, teamwork, labs, course materials, and professional professors will help you inside and outside the class to learn and prepare you for the real world. Also, all the professors have real-world experience, which they implement in their course material that is well-prepared to help you understand the material and know what to expect from you.

System Administrator

A System Administrator will learn and be responsible for installing and configuring software and hardware. Update systems when new versions are released, manage networks like firewalls, create and manage accounts, and make sure everything is secure, backups, and firmware are working properly within the infrastructure. You will use different software such as PowerShell, Azure, and FileZilla. As well as operating systems such as Windows and Linux. The four required courses with this track are IT341 Introduction to System Administration, IT442 Windows System Administration, IT443 Network Security Administration, and IT444 Network Services Administration. All of these will prepare you to become a professional system administrator. The minimum salary is $54,748 a year, the average salary is $89,066, and the highest is $144,897 a year. If all these match your preference and interest, then the system administrator is for you. on.

Information Architecture

An information architect will learn and be responsible for creating the information architecture of applications, websites, and other digital goods to improve user accessibility, usability, and findability. To make it easier for users to find and understand the content they require, they work to provide clear and simple navigation systems, including models, diagrams, sitemaps, labeling, category schemes, and search functionality. You will use software such as Enterprise, UXtweak, maze, and Loop11. The main courses required are IT360 Enterprise software, IT428 Information Security, IT461 System Analysis, and one course of your choice from IT456 Information Storage and Management, IT460 Integration Methodologies and tools, and IT471 Data Warehousing for business intelligence. All of these will prepare you to become a professional Information Architecture. The minimum salary is $75,000 a year, the average salary is $112,000, and the highest is $172,000 a year. If all these match your preference and interest, then the information architecture is for you.

Business Intelligence

Business Intelligence will learn and be responsible for the technological framework that gathers, stores, and evaluates corporate data. By converting raw data into useful insights, business intelligence gives managers the data and reports they need to make wise business choices. You will use software such as Oracle, Zoho Analytics, Qlik Sense, and Tableau. The main courses required are IT 370 Business Intelligence, IT 471 Data Warehousing, IT 472 Data Mining, and you can choose two of the following elective courses: IT 360 Enterprise Software, IT 428L Information System Security, IT 456 Storage Management, IT 460 Integration Methodologies and Tools, IT 461L Systems Analysis and Designs 422 Decision Support Systems, MSIS 426 E-Business and E-Commerce, MSIS 427 Knowledge Management. Leaving you with many course choices to choose from since it's a big field. The minimum salary is $57,000 a year, the average salary is $76,000, and the highest is $104,000 a year. If all these match your preference and interest, then the Business intelligence is for you.

Computer Forensics

Computer Forensics will learn and be responsible for digital forensics. Such as computer forensics, which assists in locating, gathering, and protecting evidence from an electronic device through the use of technology and investigative methods. Businesses and individuals can employ computer forensics to recover lost or corrupted data, and law enforcement authorities can utilize it in court. There are different types of forensics. such as database forensics, malware forensics, network forensics, mobile forensics, memory forensics, and more. You will use software such as Encase, Wireshark, and Magnet RAM. The courses required are SOCIOL104L Intro to Criminal Justice, IT220 Computer Forensics I, IT221 Computer Forensics II, IT420 Network and Mobile Forensics, IT421 Malware Analysis, and SOCIOL364 Cyber Crime The minimum salary is $65,000 a year, the average salary is $102,672 and the highest is $156,000 a year. If all these match your preference and interest, then Computer Forensics is for you.
